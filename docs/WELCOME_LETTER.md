# Joe & Cami — Meet Your New Shop 🏁

Dear Joe and Cami,

**Put down the sync cable. Cancel the support ticket. Stop paying three companies to run one garage.**

For years you've juggled **NAPA TRACS** on the shop computer, **QuickBooks** for the books, and a little gremlin called **Accounting Link** whose entire job was carrying numbers from one to the other — and occasionally dropping them on the floor. Three programs. Three logins. Three monthly bills. One headache.

**Those days are over.** We built you something better. Not "a little better." Better like a lift is better than a creeper.

---

## ONE system. ONE login. ZERO syncing. 💥

Here's the magic trick, and it's not even a trick: **when the invoice is the bookkeeping, there's nothing to sync.** Cami invoices a customer and the income, sales tax, accounts receivable, and tax reports update *that second*. Accounting Link isn't replaced — it's **extinct**. There is no "end of day transfer." There is no "did it post to QuickBooks?" There is only *done*.

And it runs in a web browser — the shop PC, the laptop at home, **the phone in Joe's pocket under a lift**. No servers to babysit, no updates to install, no "TRACS is down again."

## No password gymnastics — just your PIN 🔢

No "Was it the email with the capital J?" The screen shows a keypad. Joe taps his 6 digits, Cami taps hers, you're in. Behind that friendly keypad the vault door is still bank-grade — your customer list and tax numbers are locked to the two of you.

## But wait — there's more. A LOT more. 🎪

**🚗 The VIN Decoder.** Paste a VIN, click decode, and the year, make, model, and engine fill themselves in — straight from the federal database, free, forever. TRACS made you type. We made it *appear*.

**📸 Digital Inspections customers can actually SEE.** Tech walks the car, rates everything green/yellow/red, snaps photos — and you text the customer **a link**. They open it on their phone, no login, see the cracked belt with their own eyes, and say *yes* to the work. That's not a feature TRACS's paper inspection sheet can even dream about. Trust goes up. Approvals go up. Average ticket goes up.

**💰 The Follow-Ups page — your "money on the table" list.** Every morning it tells you exactly who to call: estimates sitting unanswered, recommended work nobody sold, invoices past due — each with a **tap-to-call phone number** and a one-click "Log call" so you two never double-dial the same customer. TRACS *stored* that money. This **chases** it.

**📊 Shop Health — your books, in English.** Not a 40-column report. Actual sentences: *"You finished 12 cars this week — 3 more than last week."* *"You billed $6,100 but collected $5,200 — $900 is still out there, here's who to call."* *"Parts margin is thin — check your pricing matrix."* It doesn't just report the problem; it points at the button that fixes it.

**🏷️ A pricing matrix that reprices EVERYTHING.** Set your markup brackets once. Every part prices itself the moment you type its cost — and when you change the matrix, one click reprices the *entire catalog*. TRACS priced new parts. We re-price your whole shelf.

**📅 The appointment book you already know** — the same week-at-a-glance grid you use in TRACS, with every job showing the customer, the vehicle, the notes, and even those "JOE, ATTENTION" reminder blocks (they're amber now, and prettier).

**🧾 A Repair Order that outworks the paper one.** Complaint → Cause → Correction, parts and labor, signature lines — everything TRACS printed — *plus* the digital inspection summary and the customer's photo link embedded right on the ticket, and recommended future work that never falls off the page.

## And now, the part you asked for: TAX SEASON. 😱➡️😎

This was the big wish, so we went overboard:

- **Your Schedule C fills ITSELF.** Every line, computed from the invoices and expenses you already entered by doing your normal jobs. April becomes a *print button*.
- **The Year-End Tax Packet.** One click → one printable bundle with everything your tax preparer needs: Schedule C, 1099 contractor list, quarterly payments, mileage, depreciation. Hand it over. Look smug.
- **Quarterly estimates, calculated for you** — with the IRS due dates and safe-harbor math so you never eat an underpayment penalty.
- **1099 watchdog.** It *notices* when you've paid a contractor over $600 and nags you for the W-9 in July, when it's easy — not January, when it's misery.
- **Bank statement import.** Cami downloads the monthly card statement, drags the file in, the app sorts expenses into the right tax categories. Five minutes a month instead of an evening of typing.

## "Sounds great. But switching sounds painful." — NOPE. 🎯

We wrote the entire escape plan for you, in plain English, with checkboxes:

1. **The Owner's Guide** (`docs/ONBOARDING.md`) walks you through go-live step by step — what to export from TRACS and QuickBooks, and what order to type it in.
2. **The one-page Quick Start** (`docs/QUICKSTART.md`) — print it, put it by the register, check things off.
3. The **VIN decoder** turns vehicle entry from the worst part of switching into the fastest.
4. And the safety net: **run both systems side by side for a week or two.** Nothing gets turned off until you've watched this one work with your own eyes.

Setup is an afternoon. Data entry is a few coffee-fueled sessions — and it's a great excuse to finally purge the customers who moved away in 2019.

Your shop. Your data. Your software. **Nobody's renting it back to you.**
